## [TERM-001] GPU & VRAM

**GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)**
A dedicated chip for processing graphics and parallel computation. A higher-tier GPU means smoother gaming performance and faster AI model training.

**VRAM (Video RAM)**
Memory dedicated to the GPU. Critical for two things:
- Gaming: higher resolution and texture quality demand more VRAM
- AI/Deep Learning: the size of the model that can run is limited by available VRAM

VRAM guide for AI workloads:
- 8GB VRAM: sufficient for 7B parameter models (quantized)
- 12GB VRAM: comfortable for 13B models, medium dataset training
- 16GB+: ideal for fine-tuning and large models

**RTX 4080 Laptop GPU**
NVIDIA's top-tier laptop GPU (2024). Approximately 85% of desktop RTX 4080 performance. 12GB GDDR6 VRAM. Capable of running AAA games at 2K ultra settings and training ML models locally.

**Adreno 650**
The mobile GPU in the Qualcomm Snapdragon 865 Plus. Designed for smartphones — power-efficient and fast for mobile games, but not comparable to laptop GPUs for PC gaming.

## [TERM-002] Processor (CPU)

**Snapdragon 865 Plus**
Qualcomm's 2020 flagship Android chipset. 7nm fabrication, integrated 5G. Very fast for mobile gaming. Used in the ROG Phone 3.

**AMD Ryzen 9 8945HS**
AMD's 2024 flagship laptop processor. 8 cores / 16 threads, 4nm fabrication. Highly efficient and powerful for heavy computation, code compilation, and multitasking. Used in the ROG Zephyrus G14 2025.

## [TERM-003] RAM

**RAM (Random Access Memory)**
The system's working memory. More RAM means more applications can run simultaneously without slowdown.

**LPDDR5 vs LPDDR5X**
- LPDDR5: 5th generation mobile/laptop RAM. High bandwidth, power-efficient.
- LPDDR5X: Extended version of LPDDR5, approximately 33% higher bandwidth. Used in the Zephyrus G14.

RAM guide:
- 8GB: sufficient for light gaming and everyday work
- 16GB: modern gaming standard
- 32GB: ideal for AI/data science, video editing, heavy multitasking

## [TERM-004] Storage

**UFS (Universal Flash Storage)**
The storage standard for smartphones. UFS 3.1 (in the ROG Phone 3) achieves read speeds of approximately 2100 MB/s — very fast for mobile standards.

**NVMe SSD (PCIe Gen 4)**
The fastest storage standard for laptops and PCs. PCIe Gen 4 in the Zephyrus G14 reaches approximately 7000 MB/s read — far faster than HDD (150 MB/s) or SATA SSD (550 MB/s). Game loading and boot times become extremely fast.

## [TERM-005] Display

**AMOLED**
Organic display technology. Deep blacks (pixels fully off), vivid colors, high contrast. Ideal for gaming and media. Used in the ROG Phone 3 (6.59 inch, 144Hz).

**OLED vs AMOLED**
Essentially the same. AMOLED is Samsung's variant of OLED. Both offer premium image quality.

**144Hz vs 120Hz**
The refresh rate — how many times per second the display updates.
- 144Hz (ROG Phone 3): smoother for competitive mobile gaming
- 120Hz (Zephyrus G14): premium laptop standard; the difference from 144Hz is barely noticeable at 14 inches

**3K Resolution (2880×1800)**
Sharper than Full HD (1920×1080) and nearly equivalent to 4K, but more power-efficient. Ideal for a 14-inch laptop due to very high pixel density.

## [TERM-006] Connectivity

**5G**
Fifth-generation cellular network. The Snapdragon 865 Plus in the ROG Phone 3 supports Sub-6GHz 5G.

**30W Fast Charging**
Rapid charging technology. The ROG Phone 3 with its 6000mAh battery can fully charge in approximately 2 hours with the included 30W charger.

---

## [TERM-007] Special Gaming Features

**AirTrigger 3 (ROG Phone 3)**
Ultrasonic touch buttons on the side of the body that function as additional triggers during landscape gaming. Enables more precise control without thumbs covering the screen.

**AeroActive Cooler (ROG Phone accessory)**
An external cooler that attaches to the back of the ROG Phone. Adds an active fan and an external USB-C port. Strongly recommended for gaming sessions over 1 hour to keep battery temperature stable.

**ROG Intelligent Cooling + Liquid Metal (Zephyrus G14)**
The laptop's active cooling system using liquid metal (rather than standard thermal paste) as the CPU heat conductor. Lower temperatures mean more stable performance during long gaming sessions (no thermal throttling).
