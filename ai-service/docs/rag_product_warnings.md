# PRODUCT WARNINGS & REALISTIC EXPECTATIONS — GADGET SOLUTION

> This document contains honest product limitations that MUST be communicated
> before a customer decides to buy — especially when their usage habits may
> conflict with a product's known limitations.
> Goal: build long-term trust and prevent post-purchase regret.

---

## [WARNING-001] ASUS ROG Phone 3 — Limitations & Realistic Expectations

### Positive Points Worth Confirming
- The 144Hz display feels very smooth, but only makes a meaningful difference in games that actually support high frame rates — not all mobile games support 144fps.
- The 6000mAh battery is large, but large capacity does not mean long duration during intensive gaming — power consumption under heavy load is high.

### Limitations That Must Be Communicated

**Heating during extended gaming:**
The ROG Phone 3 is designed for maximum performance, not power efficiency. After 45–60 minutes of continuous gaming without a break, the body temperature can rise significantly (especially around the center back). Without the AeroActive Cooler, this can cause discomfort during long sessions and trigger thermal throttling (automatic performance reduction to protect temperature).

**Size and weight:**
With a 6.59-inch display and 6000mAh battery, the ROG Phone 3 is larger and heavier than the average smartphone. Not ideal as a primary phone for users accustomed to compact devices.

**Camera is not a priority:**
The ROG Phone 3 is not designed for photography. The camera is functional but does not compare to flagship camera phones like the Samsung Galaxy S-series or iPhone. If the customer frequently captures photos or videos for content, this needs to be communicated.

**No MicroSD slot:**
The 128GB storage cannot be expanded with a memory card. For users who store many videos or large games, 128GB may feel limiting over time.

**Accessories are sold separately:**
The AeroActive Cooler, Kunai Gamepad, and TwinView Dock are all sold separately. Total cost can increase significantly if the customer wants a complete gaming setup.

### This product is NOT suitable for customers who:
- Are looking for the best camera phone for content creation
- Want a small and lightweight device
- Dislike phones that heat up during extended use
- Need a MicroSD slot for storage expansion
- Rarely game — the premium gaming features go to waste

### This product is IDEAL for customers who:
- Are competitive mobile gamers who play 1–3 hours per day
- Plan to use the AeroActive Cooler for extended sessions
- Need a highly responsive display and loud front-facing speakers

## [WARNING-002] ASUS ROG Zephyrus G14 2025 — Limitations & Realistic Expectations

### Positive Points Worth Confirming
- The RTX 4080 Laptop GPU is very powerful, but there is still approximately a 15% performance gap compared to the full desktop RTX 4080 — normal for a laptop form factor.
- The 3K OLED display is stunning, but OLED carries a long-term burn-in risk if static elements are displayed continuously (taskbar, game HUD).

### Limitations That Must Be Communicated

**Heavy for a 14-inch laptop:**
With an aggressive cooling system and large battery, the Zephyrus G14 is heavier than a typical ultrabook. Customers planning to carry it daily to campus or the office should factor this in.

**Battery life during gaming:**
When running at full performance with the GPU active, battery life can drop dramatically to just 1.5–2.5 hours. For serious gaming, being plugged in is practically mandatory. Long battery life (8–10 hours) only applies during light work or browsing.

**On-board RAM cannot be upgraded:**
32GB is already substantial, but since the RAM is soldered directly to the motherboard (on-board), it cannot be expanded later. This is a long-term commitment worth considering.

**Premium price:**
IDR 34,500,000 is a significant investment. Customers need to be confident they will genuinely utilize the RTX 4080 and 32GB RAM — not just for browsing and Office work.

**Very limited stock:**
Only 2 units remaining. If the customer is still undecided and delays their decision, stock may run out. Communicate this honestly without applying pressure.

**14-inch display for AAA gaming:**
Some gamers find 14 inches too small for an immersive AAA gaming experience. Customers used to gaming on 24-inch or larger monitors should adjust their expectations — or consider connecting to an external monitor.

### This product is NOT suitable for customers who:
- Only need a laptop for Office, browsing, and Zoom — massive overkill at this price
- Travel frequently and are sensitive to bag weight
- Want to upgrade RAM in the future
- Expect to game for long sessions without being plugged in
- Have a budget below IDR 30 million

### This product is IDEAL for customers who:
- Are serious gamers playing AAA titles at high resolutions
- Are AI, data science, or machine learning students or professionals
- Are content creators who need fast rendering and editing
- Want one laptop that handles all demanding tasks (all-in-one powerhouse)
- Want a powerful laptop that still maintains a relatively thin form factor

## [WARNING-003] How to Communicate Limitations to Customers

Product limitations must be communicated in a way that builds trust — not one that frightens customers or creates unnecessary doubt.

### The Right Approach

**Only mention limitations relevant to the customer's stated usage habits.**
If the customer says they don't care about the camera, there's no need to dwell on the ROG Phone 3's camera limitations.

**Use a positive-negative-positive sandwich:**
1. Acknowledge the product's strength first
2. State the relevant limitation honestly
3. Close by checking whether that limitation is actually a problem for this customer

Example:
> "The ROG Phone 3 has an incredibly smooth display for gaming. One thing worth knowing — without an extra cooler, temperatures can rise after about an hour of play. How long do your typical gaming sessions usually run?"

**Frame limitations as a question, not a verdict:**
Instead of: *"This phone has a poor camera"*
Better: *"The camera is fairly standard for a gaming phone. Is photography something important to you as well?"*

### What to Avoid
- Never conceal limitations in order to seem like you're recommending the best product
- Never dump all limitations at once — choose only the most relevant ones
- Never use words like "bad", "broken", or "failure" — use "limitation" or "worth considering"
- Never let a customer decide to buy without knowing at least one key product limitation
